# BIOE_276_Humpback_Whale_Activity_Budgets
**Group final project**
## The Data: 
  Using data from Customized Animal Tracking Solutions (CATS) and Digital Acoustic Recording (DTAG) suction-cup tag deployments on humpback whales near the Western Antarctic Peninsula across 2012-2025. Looking at 34 individual and 4 mom+calf pairs of humpback whales to see if there is variation between the two groups in their behavior/time spent. Other whales were tagged, but not all data had been processed, resulting in unrepresentative data that, if used, would have disrupted the results. It is also important to note that we had no control over which whales were tagged, and, if possible, we would have had equal numbers of mom-calf pairs and individuals. 
## Our Question:
  How does the presence of a calf affect the behavior of a whale during foraging season along the Western Antarctic Peninsula?
## Our Hypothesis:
  The presence of a calf will cause the behavior of a tagged whale to vary, resulting in different proportions of time exploring, feeding, traveling, and resting.
